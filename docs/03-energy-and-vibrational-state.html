Module 03 Building

Energy & Awareness Work and the Vibrational State

Learn to raise and move energy/awareness through the body and to recognize, induce, and steady the vibrational state that practitioners describe just before separation — and to control it well enough to send a wave from your head to your toes on command. We stop there. Leaving the body is Module 05. This module ends with you sitting in the vibrations, in control, not exiting.

By the end you can

  • Run an awareness-circulation / energy-raising routine — Robert Bruce's tactile imaging — moving felt attention through body zones to intensify sensation deliberately.
  • Recognize the vibrational state (buzzing, tingling, electrical, wave-like, full-body) and induce it from the hypnagogic threshold, Monroe-style.
  • Control the vibrations — push them into the head, drive them to the toes, and roll a head-to-foot wave on command — then stop cleanly at "in control," not exiting.
  • Hold the grounded frame: these sensations are real and reportable, tied to the threshold / REM atonia state, and not dangerous — keeping experiential, evidenced, and interpretive claims distinct.

Read this first

A note on the word "energy"

Throughout this module you'll see the word energy. We use it the way practitioners use it — as a name for a felt thing: the localized tingling, pressure, buzzing, warmth, and "fizz" you can deliberately produce by paying a certain kind of attention to a part of your body. That felt thing is real and reliably reportable. Almost anyone can produce it within a few minutes of trying.

What we are not doing is asserting that this proves a literal, externally measurable energy flowing in channels. The honest position, held all the way through, keeps three lenses apart:

  • experiential Real-to-you, reportable: the tingling, the buzz, the wave. You will feel these. So do most people who try. That is not in dispute.
  • evidenced Measurable, third-person: attention demonstrably changes bodily sensation. Sustained, focused attention on a body part heightens and can amplify sensation there. The threshold state near sleep (Module 02) comes with its own buzzing, electrical, and floating sensations tied to the brain partially entering REM atonia while you stay aware.
  • interpretive A framing, not a fact: that what you feel is a subtle "energy body" with centers and conduits. This is the tradition's map. A useful map for organizing the practice — but a map, not a verified territory.

★ The tradition holds

When Robert Bruce says "move your awareness through your thumb and feel the energy center activate," he means you are stimulating a real energy body — centers and conduits that awareness-in-motion lights up and feeds.

◎ The grounded view

The same instruction, translated: move your felt attention through your thumb, and you'll feel sensation rise there. Both sentences point at the identical experience. You can run the entire practice believing the tradition's map, or none of it, and it works the same.

◎ The grounded frame, kept honest

"Energy" here is shorthand for felt attention and the sensation it amplifies — nothing in this module asserts a literal, measurable energy current. Keep the three lenses above separate in your own head and you'll never be misled, in either direction: you won't dismiss a real sensation, and you won't inflate a tingle into a metaphysical certainty.

Lesson 1

What this stage is, and where it sits on the path

By now you can relax the body completely (Module 01), notice subtle sensation without flinching (also 01), and find and hold the hypnagogic threshold — "mind awake, body asleep," what the Monroe tradition calls Focus 10 — using correct sleep timing and WBTB (Module 02). You have a platform and an edge to stand on. This module adds the engine. It teaches two linked skills:

  1. Energy / awareness work — Robert Bruce's New Energy Ways and its core tool, tactile imaging: moving felt attention (not visualization) through zones of the body to deliberately raise and circulate sensation. A warm-up and an amplifier that trains the exact attentional muscle the rest of the practice depends on.
  2. The vibrational state — Robert Monroe's name for the buzzing, tingling, electrical, wave-like, full-body sensation many projectors report washing over them right before separation. You'll learn to recognize it, induce it on purpose, and — the heart of this module — control it.

Energy/awareness work is most accessible from the relaxed threshold you built in 02, which is exactly why it comes after it. The vibrational state, in turn, is the doorway state for exit techniques. So this is the last of the "Building" rung — you're assembling the loaded spring. Module 04 teaches you to hold focus on this whole stack without falling asleep; Module 05 teaches you to use this control to actually leave. Not yet. Today's finish line is: vibrations, induced and steered, calmly held.

⚠ A reassurance you'll hear repeatedly

None of this is dangerous. The buzzing can be loud, even startling the first time — people describe an electrical roar, a body-wide tremor, a sense of being plugged into a wall socket. It is intense, not harmful: your nervous system at the edge of sleep, plus focused attention, producing a vivid sensation. You are lying safely in bed the entire time. Nothing is "coming out." The single biggest skill this module teaches isn't generating the vibrations — it's staying calm and curious inside them. experiential

Lesson 2

Tactile imaging: feeling, not picturing

The foundation of Bruce's system is a distinction that trips up most beginners: tactile imaging is not visualization. You are not picturing light, color, or glowing centers. You are using your sense of touch, from the inside — your felt body awareness — and moving it.

Try it right now, eyes open: put your attention inside your right thumb. Not looking at it — feeling it from within, the way you'd feel a splinter without looking. Most people feel a faint presence, a dull inner "thereness." That felt presence is your point of awareness. Tactile imaging is simply taking that point and giving it motion.

Bruce's core move is the brushing action: take your point of awareness and brush it slowly up and down through your thumb — from the base joint to the tip of the nail and back — feeling the whole inner pathway as you sweep through it. Do this for thirty seconds to a couple of minutes. Most people start to feel a localized heaviness, pressure, tingling, buzzing, or a deep inner "tickle" building inside the thumb.

★ Tradition's framing

You've stimulated the energy center in the thumb into higher activity; awareness-in-motion is what moves and raises energy. interpretive

◎ Grounded framing

Sustained, moving, focused attention on a body part amplifies the sensation you feel there. You're turning up the gain on interoception — your inner body sense. evidenced

Both descriptions predict the same thing, and the thing happens. That's the whole technique. Everything else in NEW is this one move — move felt attention through a body zone — applied to bigger and bigger areas.

Why start at the thumb? Because it's small, easy, and gives a fast, unmistakable result, which teaches your body what "success" feels like. Once you can reliably raise sensation in a thumb, you scale up.

Lesson 3

Primary centers, the bounce, and scaling up

Bruce holds that the hands and the soles of the feet are the body's largest energy conduits — the areas richest in what he calls energy-exchange ports — and therefore the best places to develop awareness sensitivity early. interpretive The grounded translation: hands and feet are densely innervated and richly represented in the brain's body map, so they're the easiest places to feel strong sensation. evidenced Same practical advice, either way. So after the thumb, you practice on whole hands and whole feet.

Two actions you'll use, both just "moving felt attention" with different shapes:

  • The sweep (brushing): move your point of awareness in a long, smooth pass through a zone — e.g., a slow upward sweep from the soles of the feet up to the hips, about three seconds per sweep, repeated. This warms and "lights up" a whole limb or section.
  • The bounce: move awareness rapidly back and forth through a body part — a quick oscillation rather than a long stroke. Bouncing in place tends to build sensation faster and more intensely than slow sweeping. You can speed up or slow the bounce to change how strongly the area "fizzes."

As you scale up, you're working toward a full-body circuit: sweeping and bouncing awareness through both feet, both legs, hands, arms, the trunk, the head — circulating that raised sensation around the whole body rather than leaving it pooled in one spot. Bruce also describes vitality storage centers in the trunk (a sub-navel center below the belly button; a sub-heart/solar-plexus center at the base of the sternum; a sub-brow center) where raised energy can be gathered and "stored." For our purposes you don't need the chakra-style map — you need the skill: take felt attention and move it, smoothly or rapidly, through any zone you choose, and feel sensation rise and travel.

That skill — steering felt attention around the body and intensifying it on demand — is exactly what the vibrational-state work needs. Energy work is the gym; the vibrational state is the match.

Lesson 4

Raising and intensifying sensation deliberately

Recognizing sensation is Module 01. Cranking it up on purpose is the new skill here. Three levers:

  1. Dwell longer. Sensation builds with sustained attention. A zone that feels faint after five seconds often roars after forty. Most beginners quit too early.
  2. Move the attention (sweep/bounce) rather than holding it still. Motion recruits more of the area and keeps the sensation from fading into the background — attention habituates to anything perfectly static.
  3. Breathe with it. Many practitioners pair a slow inhale with "drawing" sensation into a zone and the exhale with letting it spread or settle. The breath isn't magic; it gives the attention a rhythm to ride and keeps arousal low.

The target feeling as you intensify: tingling that thickens into buzzing, pressure that becomes a kind of inner vibration, warmth that spreads. When you can take a faint tingle in your feet and, over a minute, build it into a strong, obvious buzz that you could "turn up" further — you have the lever the vibrational state runs on.

✦ Important restraint

Do all of this without straining. The harder you clench mentally, the more you raise arousal and the more you push the threshold — and the sensation — away. Intensifying is a matter of patient, sustained, relaxed attention, not effort. This is the single most common beginner error, and it has its own entry in Common Mistakes.

Lesson 5

Recognizing the vibrational state

Now the destination. Robert Monroe popularized the term vibrational state in Journeys Out of the Body (1971), describing it as the most important and characteristic precursor to leaving the body — a sensation that, for him, would sweep in unbidden as he lay at the edge of sleep.

How practitioners describe it — this is experiential, what's reported, real-to-them, varied between people:

  • A buzzing or humming through the whole body.
  • Tingling, like fizz or static, or pins-and-needles spread everywhere at once.
  • An electrical feeling — "as if a mild current is being shot through me," a body-wide hum like holding a low-voltage line.
  • A wave-like or rushing/roaring quality — for some it pulses or rolls; for some it's accompanied by a loud internal sound (buzzing, roaring, ringing).
  • A sense of the body being rigid, heavy, or "locked," sometimes with rapid internal trembling.

★ The tradition holds

The vibrations are the energy/second body loosening and charging — the felt signature of the subtle body preparing to separate. interpretive

◎ The grounded view

You reach this state at the hypnagogic threshold: the body slides into sleep, the muscles enter or near atonia (the natural paralysis that stops you acting out dreams), while awareness stays switched on. That mismatch — numbing body, awake mind — produces strong, strange, vivid sensations. Buzzing, electrical surges, vibrating, floating, and a "locked" body are classic, well-documented features of this borderland and of sleep paralysis, which is the same machinery. evidenced

So: the vibrational state is real (you feel it), reliably reportable (many people independently describe the same cluster), tied to a known physiological state (threshold / REM atonia), and not dangerous (an intense sensation, not an injury, with your body safe in bed). Whether it's also "energy" or "the second body loosening" is the interpretive layer — believe it or not, the practice is identical.

✦ The recognition cue you're listening for

A shift from localized raised sensation (a buzzing hand, fizzing feet) to a whole-body hum, tingle, or electrical wash that arrives somewhat on its own once you're deep enough at the threshold. When the buzzing stops being "in my feet because I'm working on my feet" and becomes "everywhere, and bigger than my effort" — that's the vibrational state announcing itself.

Lesson 6

Inducing the vibrational state (the Monroe-style approach)

You induce the vibrational state by combining everything so far: get to the threshold, then point and hold attention in a way that lets the buzz build and generalize. The Monroe-style induction, adapted faithfully and stated in grounded terms:

  1. Get to the edge. Relax fully (01), reach the hypnagogic threshold with good timing — ideally a WBTB session (02). The vibrational state is far more accessible here than from cold wakefulness. You cannot force it from an alert, daytime body; you coax it from the edge of sleep.
  2. Establish "mind awake, body asleep." Keep a thin thread of awareness alive (the count, the held image, the noticing stance from 02) while the body goes limp and numb. Let the body fade.
  3. Reach out with attention. Monroe's image was directing attention to a point in the space just beyond the body and "drawing" the vibration toward you; many teachers instead have you focus on a region of the body (often the head, the spine, or the whole body at once) and invite sensation there. Either way: fix relaxed attention and stay open to the buzz rising. Don't manufacture it by clenching — allow and amplify it, the way you amplified the thumb.
  4. Let it generalize. When tingling or buzzing appears anywhere, gently widen your attention to include the whole body, inviting the sensation to spread until it's a full-body hum. Your energy-work warm-up makes this dramatically easier — a body you've already "lit up" slides into the vibrational state much more readily.
  5. Stay flat and calm. The instant the vibrations arrive, the natural reaction is a jolt of excitement or fear — and that jolt raises arousal and evaporates the state (you've left the threshold by waking up). The induction isn't complete until you can let the vibrations come and keep lying there, unbothered, observing. This calm is the skill. Practice it deliberately.

A realistic expectation: you may need many attempts. Some sessions you'll reach a strong buzz; some you'll get a flicker; some, nothing, and you'll just have practiced relaxing at the threshold (never wasted). A "failed" induction still trains the platform.

Lesson 7 · the keystone

Controlling the vibrations (the core skill of this module)

Once vibrations are present, Monroe's instruction is to take charge of them — and this control is precisely what later becomes an exit trigger (in Module 05), so it's worth drilling now until it's clean. Monroe's classic sequence:

  1. Concentrate on the vibrations as a single whole-body phenomenon — feel them as one field, not scattered patches.
  2. Push them into your head. Mentally gather and drive the buzzing up so it concentrates and intensifies in your head. With practice the whole-body buzz will surge and crest there.
  3. Drive them down to your toes. Then push that concentrated sensation down the length of the body, all the way to the feet.
  4. Roll a wave, head to foot. Now oscillate: push the wave up into the head, then sweep it back down to the toes, then up again — producing vibrational waves rolling from head to foot and back. Speed them up, slow them down, make them surge through the whole body on command.

The control move is the same felt-attention steering you trained in tactile imaging — only now the medium is the body-wide vibrational hum instead of a quiet tingle in one limb. Sweeping awareness from soles to hips (Lesson 3) is the rehearsal; rolling the vibrational wave from head to foot is the performance.

★ Tradition's framing

You're learning to direct the energy body — the prerequisite for projecting it. interpretive

◎ Grounded framing

You're learning fine attentional control over a vivid threshold-state body sensation — steering where the amplified feeling concentrates. A real, trainable skill of attention over interoception that keeps you engaged and calm in a state that otherwise tends to fade or spook you. evidenced

✦ Why this is the module's keystone

Monroe taught that once you can induce and control the vibrations on command, you're ready to leave the body — separation grows directly out of this control. We are not taking that last step yet. But by the end of this module you should be able to sit in the vibrational state and roll a wave from your crown to your toes deliberately, several times, while staying calm. That is mastery of this rung.

Lesson 8

Holding the grounded frame throughout

Carry these four points into every session:

  1. The sensations are real and reportable. You're not imagining the buzz in a dismissive sense; you're genuinely feeling it. Trust it. experiential
  2. They're tied to a known state. Threshold + REM-atonia + focused attention. That's why they show up where they do (the edge of sleep) and feel the way they do (electrical, locked, floating, buzzing). evidenced
  3. They are not dangerous. Intense ≠ harmful. Your body is safe in bed.
  4. Keep the three lenses separate. "I feel a body-wide electrical buzz" (experiential). "This matches threshold/REM-atonia physiology" (evidenced). "This is my energy body loosening for projection" (interpretive). All three can coexist; only the first two are claims this course stands behind, and you never have to adopt the third to do the practice.

⚠ The Module 00 guidance still stands

If you have any of the conditions flagged in Module 00 — psychosis / schizophrenia-spectrum, dissociative disorders, severe anxiety or panic — defer this intensive work or check with a professional first.

Worked examples

Two sessions, step by step

A — A full tactile-imaging energy warm-up (≈10 minutes)

Context: lying supine in your practice posture, ideally after the relaxation routine from Module 01. This is the "lighting the body up" warm-up you'll run before vibrational-state work. Read it once, then do it from memory — eyes closed, no screen.

  1. Settle (1 min). Three slow diaphragmatic breaths (01). Let the body get heavy. Decide: "I'm warming up the body's sensation, calmly. No goal beyond that."
  2. Thumb (1 min). Put your point of awareness inside your right thumb. Brush it slowly up and down — base joint to nail tip and back — feeling the whole inner pathway. Wait for the heaviness/tingle/buzz to build. Note the moment it "switches on." That's your calibration of success.
  3. Right hand (1 min). Expand the same brushing to the whole right hand — palm, fingers, back of the hand. Then bounce: oscillate awareness rapidly back and forth across the hand for a few seconds and feel the buzz jump up. Let the hand "fizz."
  4. Left hand (1 min). Same: brush, then bounce, until it matches the right.
  5. Both feet (2 min). Drop awareness to the soles of the feet (Bruce's prime conduit). Brush each sole, then bounce both feet together. These often "light up" strongly — give them time.
  6. Sweep the legs (2 min). Slow upward sweeps, soles to hips, ~3 seconds per sweep, repeated — first the right leg, then the left, then both together. Feel a moving band of warmth/tingle travel up each pass.
  7. Trunk and up to the head (1 min). Sweep awareness up the front of the torso — past the sub-navel, the solar-plexus center, the chest — up the throat and into the head. Let the raised sensation pool and gather as it rises.
  8. Whole-body circuit (1 min). Sweep awareness in a slow loop: up the front to the head, down the back to the feet, and around again — circulating the lit-up sensation through the whole body as one connected circuit. Finish by just lying in the overall buzz you've created.

Outcome to expect: a body that feels distinctly "switched on," tingling or humming in patches or as a whole, far more sensation-rich than when you started. You haven't tried to project anything. You've built the engine and trained the steering. This warm-up alone, repeated, is the bulk of the practice at this stage.

B — Vibrational-state induction and control session (WBTB)

Context: a Wake-Back-To-Bed session (02) — you woke after ~5 hours, were up briefly, and returned to bed with intention. The body is primed to fall back asleep fast, which is exactly what you want. Here's the full session script:

Avoid these

Common mistakes

⚠ Common mistakes

  1. Straining / forcing the sensation. Clenching your mind to "make" the buzz happen raises arousal, which both pulls you off the sleep threshold and suppresses the very sensation you're chasing. Fix: relaxed, patient, sustained attention. Think "allow and amplify," not "force." Vibrations rise into relaxed attention; they retreat from effort.
  2. Fear of the buzzing. The vibrations can feel intense, electrical, even like a body-wide tremor or roar — and a first-timer's instinct can be alarm ("am I being electrocuted? is this a seizure?"). Fix: know in advance it's coming and that it's a harmless threshold/REM-atonia sensation. Meet it with "expected, harmless, I'm safe in bed," and keep observing. (Deeper fear / sleep-paralysis work is Module 06.)
  3. Getting excited and losing the state. The flip side of fear: the buzz arrives, you think "IT'S HAPPENING!", the jolt of excitement spikes arousal, you wake slightly, and the threshold — and the vibrations — evaporate. Fix: treat arrival as utterly ordinary. Notice the excitement, let it pass, stay flat. Boredom is your friend here.
  4. Under-interpreting / quitting too early. Feeling a faint tingle, thinking "that's nothing," and stopping — when forty more seconds of patient dwell would have built it into a real buzz. Fix: trust faint sensation as the seed; dwell and intensify. Most "nothing happened" sessions ended one minute too soon.
  5. Over-interpreting ordinary sensation. The opposite error: reading every routine tingle, twitch, or muscle flutter as "the energy body activating" and building grand metaphysical conclusions on it. Fix: keep the three lenses separate. A tingle is a tingle (experiential); whether it's "energy" is interpretive and not required.
  6. Skipping the threshold / wrong timing. Trying to induce the vibrational state from full, alert wakefulness (a daytime attempt with a wired body) and getting nowhere. Fix: the vibrational state lives at the sleep threshold. Use relaxation (01) + good timing/WBTB (02). The energy warm-up amplifies; it doesn't replace the threshold.
  7. No warm-up. Going straight for the vibrational state from a "cold," un-sensitized body. Fix: run the tactile-imaging warm-up first — a pre-lit body slides into vibrations far more readily.
